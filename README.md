# IT-Governance-Risk-and-Compliance-ITGRC

## Project Overview

This repository documents the academic learning  **IT Governance, Risk and Compliance (ITGRC)**.

It focused on understanding how organizations manage information technology responsibly through governance structures, risk management practices, and compliance mechanisms. The objective was to analyze how organizations ensure that IT systems support business goals while maintaining security, regulatory compliance, and operational resilience.


## Course Context

Modern organizations rely heavily on information technology to support their business operations. As digital systems become more complex, organizations must establish clear governance structures, manage risks effectively, and ensure compliance with internal policies and external regulations.

The ITGRC framework integrates three key components:

- **Governance** – Ensuring that IT supports organizational strategy and delivers value
- **Risk Management** – Identifying and mitigating risks that may affect information systems
- **Compliance** – Ensuring adherence to legal, regulatory, and internal policy requirements

## IT Governance

IT governance defines how organizations manage and control their IT resources. It ensures that IT investments align with business strategy and contribute to organizational performance.

Governance structures establish decision-making processes, roles, responsibilities, and accountability mechanisms that guide how IT systems are planned, implemented, and monitored.

Common governance objectives include:

- Aligning IT strategy with business strategy  
- Ensuring value delivery from IT investments  
- Managing IT-related risks  
- Monitoring IT performance and accountability  

Frameworks such as **COBIT** provide structured guidance for implementing IT governance in organizations.


## Risk Management

Risk management focuses on identifying, assessing, and mitigating risks related to information systems, data, and organizational operations.

The risk management process typically includes:

1. Identifying potential threats and vulnerabilities  
2. Assessing the likelihood and impact of risks  
3. Implementing risk mitigation or control measures  
4. Monitoring risks and continuously improving security practices  

Effective risk management helps organizations maintain business continuity and protect critical assets.

## Compliance

Compliance ensures that organizations operate according to relevant laws, regulations, and internal policies. This includes regulations related to data protection, cybersecurity, financial reporting, and industry standards.

Compliance programs typically involve:

- Policy development  
- Internal controls and procedures  
- Auditing and monitoring activities  
- Reporting and documentation  

Maintaining compliance helps organizations reduce legal risks and build trust with stakeholders.


## Governance Frameworks and Standards

During the course, several governance and compliance frameworks were studied to understand how organizations structure their IT governance processes.

Examples include:

- **COBIT (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies)** – governance and management of enterprise IT  
- **ISO/IEC 27001** – information security management systems (ISMS)  
- Risk management frameworks used for identifying and mitigating IT risks  

These frameworks provide structured approaches for managing governance, risk, and compliance within organizations.
